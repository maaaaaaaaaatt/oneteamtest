WELCOME TO GROUP LICENSING 101

Group licensing is when athletes come together and leverage their collective name, image and likeness (NIL) value to get deals done with brands. It offers an efficient and accessible way for athletes to obtain and share NIL revenue.

100% of the players in the NFL, MLB, WNBA, NBA and MLS participate in those respective sport group licensing programs.

This is how products like video games, trading cards and name and number jerseys get to market.

Group NIL rights are represented by a third party, like OneTeam, that negotiates on behalf of the athletes to maximize what a brand pays to include their NIL on retail products.

Athletes are still permitted to engage in individual NIL deals outside of the group licensing agreement.

Schools will review the use of institutional marks and logos on any co-branded products, while the third party representing the group of athletes will review the use of the athletes’ NIL.

Deals have standard terms that apply to all athletes who participate in a specific deal, which creates equal opportunity and financial success for all involved athletes.

Athletes get paid a percentage of the sale of licensed products without having to actively do anything.

Athletes will be compensated for video games that feature their likeness and jerseys in the bookstore with their name and number!
